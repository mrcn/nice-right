## 1. The Core Problem: What IS the Offer?

### Diagnosis

The user asked "what IS the offer?" -- that question alone is the most important signal. If the person selling it cannot articulate the offer in one sentence, the buyer definitely cannot. Hormozi is explicit about this: a confused buyer never buys.

### What the current page actually says

The pricing section presents four different things:

1. A $500 Conversion Audit (one-time deliverable)
2. A $1,500 Growth Audit (diagnostic credited toward a project)
3. A $5K-$15K Build & Launch (project-based custom work)
4. An $800-$2,500/mo Growth Partnership (ongoing retainer)

These are four separate services at four separate price points for four separate buyer intents. They are presented as a pricing table, but they are not a single offer. They are a menu. Hormozi explicitly warns against this: "When you give people options, you give them the option to say no."

### The identity crisis

The current structure tries to serve three different buyers simultaneously:

- **The browser** who wants a quick fix ($500 audit)
- **The buyer** who wants a full project ($5K-$15K)
- **The committed partner** who wants ongoing support ($800-$2,500/mo)

This is a classic "something for everyone" trap. It signals that Nice Right is not sure who it is for, which makes it harder for any single buyer to feel like it is specifically for them.

### What the single core transformation should be

Marcin's real value proposition, distilled from the entire site, case studies, and testimonials:

> "I turn your website from a cost into a revenue engine -- more customers finding you, more of them buying, and systems that save you time -- so your business grows without you becoming a tech expert."

That is the transformation. Everything else is a delivery mechanism for it.

### Recommended fix

Pick one primary offer. Everything else is either a stepping stone into it or a bonus within it. The Growth Audit should not be a standalone product competing with Build & Launch -- it should be the entry point into the core offer. This is exactly what Hormozi calls the "trip wire" or lead magnet that naturally escalates to the Grand Slam Offer.

## 2. Grand Slam Offer Assessment

### Hormozi's definition

"An offer combining an attractive promotion, unmatchable value proposition, premium price, and unbeatable guarantee with payment terms allowing you to get paid acquiring new customers."

The test: would a prospect "feel stupid saying no"?

### Current state: Not a Grand Slam Offer

Here is why, scored against the four components:

| Component | Current State | Score (1-10) |
|-----------|--------------|:---:|
| Attractive promotion | No promotion. No reason to buy now vs. later. | 2 |
| Unmatchable value proposition | Good services listed, but they read as commodity web dev. No unique mechanism. | 4 |
| Premium price | $5K-$15K is mid-market. Not premium, not budget. Stuck in the middle. | 5 |
| Unbeatable guarantee | No guarantee whatsoever. FAQ says "then we fix it" but that is buried and vague. | 1 |

**Overall Grand Slam Score: 3/10**

### What would make it a Grand Slam Offer

A Grand Slam Offer for Nice Right would need to:

1. **Name a specific dream outcome** -- not "a website" but "a digital presence that generates X leads per month" or "a system that saves you 15 hours per week"
2. **Include a unique mechanism** -- the proprietary process that makes this different from every other freelancer. The Growth Audit is close but is not positioned as a unique methodology.
3. **Stack visible value** -- bonuses that address every remaining objection
4. **Remove all risk** -- a guarantee so strong the prospect has nothing to lose
5. **Create genuine scarcity** -- a real reason this cannot wait

## 3. Value Equation Scoring

### The formula

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

To maximize value: increase the top, minimize the bottom.

### Scoring the current offer (1-10 scale, 10 = best)

#### Dream Outcome: 5/10

- **What is being promised?** "A complete digital presence" / "strategy-first approach" / "real results"
- **The problem**: These are vague. No specific outcome is named. Compare to "double your inbound leads in 90 days" or "save 15 hours per week with automation." The site has the proof (290% traffic, 12x leads, 80% efficiency) but those numbers appear in the Results section, completely disconnected from the pricing section. The dream outcome is not stated in the offer itself.
- **Fix**: Lead with the specific measurable outcome. "Your website will generate measurable leads within 90 days of launch, or I keep working until it does."

#### Perceived Likelihood of Achievement: 6/10

- **What builds confidence?** The 13 years experience, 100+ projects, Fortune 500 work, case studies with real numbers, LinkedIn-verified testimonials.
- **The problem**: The proof is scattered across the page. By the time the visitor reaches Pricing, they have scrolled past the results. The pricing section itself contains zero social proof -- no testimonials, no stats, no case study references. It is an island.
- **Fix**: Embed at least one result metric and one testimonial directly in or adjacent to the pricing section. "290% traffic growth for a home service company" next to the price creates a mental anchor: "$5K got someone 290% more traffic."

#### Time Delay: 4/10 (lower is better, so 4/10 = significant time delay perceived)

- **Current messaging**: "Most projects go live within 4-8 weeks" (buried in FAQ). "Bigger outcomes build over the first 2-3 months." The pricing section says nothing about timeline.
- **The problem**: The buyer sees $5K-$15K and has no idea when they will see results. The Conversion Audit says "48 hours" which is good. The Build & Launch says nothing. When there is no timeline, the brain defaults to "long."
- **Fix**: Add specific timelines to each tier. "Your Growth Audit is delivered within 10 business days." "Most sites launch in 6 weeks." "Quick wins visible within 7 days of launch." Specific beats vague every time.

#### Effort & Sacrifice: 5/10 (lower is better, so 5/10 = moderate perceived effort)

- **Current messaging**: "Regular check-ins so you always know where things stand." "Strategy-first approach based on your Growth Audit."
- **The problem**: The buyer does not know what is expected of them. How many meetings? How much input? Will they need to provide content? Photography? Logins? The less clear the effort, the more they imagine.
- **Fix**: Make it explicit. "You commit to one 30-minute call per week. I handle everything else." This is what Hormozi calls making it "Done For You" -- the highest value position on the effort spectrum.

### Current Value Equation Score

Using relative scoring (1-10):

```
Value = (5 x 6) / (6 x 5) = 30 / 30 = 1.0
```

A score of 1.0 means perceived value roughly equals perceived cost. That is not terrible, but it is not compelling. A Grand Slam Offer aims for a perceived value of 10x the price.

### Target Value Equation Score

```
Value = (9 x 8) / (3 x 2) = 72 / 6 = 12.0
```

To get there:
- Dream Outcome from 5 to 9: Name the specific transformation with proof
- Perceived Likelihood from 6 to 8: Stack social proof into the offer
- Time Delay from 6 to 3: State explicit timelines and quick wins
- Effort from 5 to 2: "You show up to calls. I do everything else."

## 4. Offer Stack Analysis

### What is an offer stack?

Hormozi defines it as the core offer plus bonuses, guarantees, scarcity, and urgency -- layered to continually expand the price-to-value gap. Each element added makes the prospect think "I am getting even MORE than I thought."

### Current stack (what exists now)

| Element | Present? | Notes |
|---------|:--------:|-------|
| Core offer | Yes | Build & Launch ($5K-$15K) |
| Bonuses | No | Zero bonuses anywhere |
| Guarantee | No | "Then we fix it" in FAQ is not a guarantee |
| Scarcity | Weak | "2 new projects per quarter" -- underdeveloped |
| Urgency | Weak | "Spots for Q2 2026 are open" -- no deadline |
| Risk reversal | Partial | "$1,500 credited toward project" is good but not framed as risk reversal |
| Social proof in offer | No | All proof is elsewhere on the page |

### What is missing: The complete offer stack

Here is what a proper Hormozi-style offer stack would look like for Nice Right, built around the Build & Launch as the core offer:

#### Core Offer: Build & Launch ($7,500-$15,000)

Full website/app/system designed and built with the Growth Blueprint methodology.

#### Bonus 1: Growth Blueprint (Value: $1,500 -- included)

Deep-dive competitive analysis, customer journey mapping, and custom digital growth roadmap. Currently this is the Growth Audit priced separately -- it should be repositioned as the mandatory first phase of every project and framed as a bonus rather than a separate product.

- **Objection it addresses**: "I do not know what I need"
- **Why it works as a bonus**: It has a clear standalone price ($1,500) that is being given as part of the project

#### Bonus 2: 90-Day Performance Dashboard (Value: $500)

Custom analytics setup with monthly performance snapshots so you can see exactly what your investment is producing. Google Analytics, Search Console, conversion tracking -- configured and explained in plain English.

- **Objection it addresses**: "How will I know if it is working?"
- **Cost to deliver**: ~2 hours of setup time

#### Bonus 3: Speed & SEO Audit at 30 Days Post-Launch (Value: $500)

A full technical review 30 days after launch to catch anything that needs tuning. Page speed optimization, mobile responsiveness check, search ranking baseline.

- **Objection it addresses**: "What if something breaks or underperforms?"
- **Cost to deliver**: ~1-2 hours

#### Bonus 4: Competitor Intelligence Report (Value: $750)

Detailed breakdown of your top 3-5 local competitors' digital presence -- what they are doing well, where they are weak, and exactly where you can win.

- **Objection it addresses**: "My competitors already have good websites"
- **Cost to deliver**: ~3 hours of research

#### Bonus 5: Content Launch Kit (Value: $500)

5 SEO-optimized blog post outlines, 10 social media caption templates, and a Google Business Profile optimization guide -- so you have momentum from day one.

- **Objection it addresses**: "What do I do after the site launches?"
- **Cost to deliver**: ~2 hours with AI-assisted workflow

#### Stack total presented to the prospect

| Component | Value |
|-----------|------:|
| Build & Launch (core) | $7,500-$15,000 |
| Growth Blueprint | $1,500 |
| 90-Day Performance Dashboard | $500 |
| 30-Day Post-Launch Audit | $500 |
| Competitor Intelligence Report | $750 |
| Content Launch Kit | $500 |
| **Total value** | **$11,250-$18,750** |
| **Your investment** | **$7,500-$15,000** |

This creates visible price-to-value discrepancy. The prospect sees $11K+ in value for $7.5K, or $18K+ in value for $15K. Every bonus is named, priced, and tied to a specific objection.

### Why each bonus matters

Hormozi says: "Each bonus should address a specific objection or obstacle. If it does not remove a reason to say no, it should not be in the stack."

The bonuses above map to the five most common objections for someone buying web services:

1. "I do not know what I need" -- Growth Blueprint
2. "How will I know if it is working?" -- Performance Dashboard
3. "What if it breaks?" -- Post-Launch Audit
4. "My competitors are ahead of me" -- Competitor Report
5. "What do I do after launch?" -- Content Launch Kit

## 5. Naming the Offer (MAGIC Formula)

### The MAGIC formula

- **M = Magnet**: A compelling reason or theme
- **A = Avatar**: Who it is for
- **G = Goal**: The dream outcome
- **I = Interval**: Time frame
- **C = Container Word**: Blueprint, Accelerator, System, etc.

### Current names -- assessment

| Current Name | Hormozi Assessment |
|---|---|
| "Conversion Audit" | Generic. Sounds like a commodity service. Every agency offers a "conversion audit." No dream outcome, no avatar, no time frame. |
| "Growth Audit" | Better than Conversion Audit, but still generic. "Growth" is overused. "Audit" implies finding problems, not delivering transformation. |
| "Build & Launch" | Describes the process, not the outcome. Nobody wants to "build and launch." They want more customers, more revenue, more time. |
| "Growth Partnership" | Decent but vague. "Partnership" is warm but says nothing specific. |

### The fundamental naming problem

Every name describes what Marcin does, not what the client gets. Hormozi is emphatic: the name should contain the benefit, not the activity.

Nobody buys a "Growth Audit." They buy clarity about where their next 50 customers are coming from. Nobody buys "Build & Launch." They buy a lead generation engine.

### Recommended names using MAGIC

Here are three tiers renamed with the Hormozi framework:

**Tier 1 (currently "Conversion Audit"):**

Apply MAGIC: Goal (more customers) + Interval (48 hours) + Container (Report/Roadmap)

Options:
- "48-Hour Revenue Roadmap"
- "Quick-Win Conversion Report"
- "The 48-Hour Fix List"

**Recommended: "48-Hour Revenue Roadmap"**

Rationale: Specific time frame creates urgency and sets expectations. "Revenue" names the dream outcome. "Roadmap" implies actionable direction, not just a list of problems.

**Tier 2 (currently "Growth Audit"):**

Apply MAGIC: Avatar (small business) + Goal (growth plan) + Container (Blueprint)

Options:
- "Digital Growth Blueprint"
- "Revenue Growth Blueprint"
- "The Growth Blueprint"

**Recommended: "Digital Growth Blueprint"**

Rationale: "Blueprint" implies something engineered and precise. "Digital Growth" is specific enough to differentiate from generic business consulting. Dropping "Audit" removes the negative connotation of being judged.

**Tier 3 (currently "Build & Launch"):**

Apply MAGIC: Goal (customers/revenue) + Interval (implied) + Container (System/Engine)

Options:
- "Customer Growth System"
- "Digital Revenue Engine"
- "The Growth Engine"

**Recommended: "Customer Growth Engine"**

Rationale: "Engine" implies something that runs continuously and generates output. "Customer Growth" names exactly what the business owner wants. This is not a website -- it is a system that produces customers.

**Tier 4 (currently "Growth Partnership"):**

Apply MAGIC: Goal (momentum) + Interval (monthly) + Container (Partnership could stay)

Options:
- "Growth Momentum Partnership"
- "Monthly Growth Accelerator"
- "Continuous Growth Partnership"

**Recommended: "Growth Accelerator"**

Rationale: "Accelerator" implies speed and upward trajectory. Clean, professional, outcome-oriented.

### Summary of naming changes

| Current | Recommended | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Conversion Audit | 48-Hour Revenue Roadmap | Time frame + outcome + direction |
| Growth Audit | Digital Growth Blueprint | Engineered plan, not judgment |
| Build & Launch | Customer Growth Engine | Outcome, not activity |
| Growth Partnership | Growth Accelerator | Speed and trajectory |

## 6. Price-to-Value Discrepancy

### Hormozi's principle

"The reason people buy anything is to get a deal. They believe what they are getting (VALUE) is worth more than what they are giving (PRICE). The larger the gap, the higher the conversion rate."

The goal is never to lower price. It is to increase perceived value until the price feels like a fraction of what is being received.

### Current pricing analysis

#### $500 Conversion Audit: Pricing is too low

- **Signal problem**: $500 for a professional audit from a 13-year veteran signals "commodity." It invites comparison with Fiverr audits at $50-$200 and automated tools at $0-$100.
- **Value problem**: The deliverable is a "prioritized list of high-impact fixes." That sounds like a glorified checklist. Where is the strategic insight? The competitive context? The revenue impact estimates?
- **Hormozi lens**: This price is so low it actually damages perceived value of everything above it. If the audit is only $500, how good can the $15K work really be?
- **Recommendation**: Either raise to $750-$1,000 and add a revenue impact estimate for each recommendation, or keep at $500 but frame it as a limited-time introductory diagnostic that exists solely to demonstrate value. The better Hormozi move: make it free but high-friction (requires a qualifying call first) and use it as the trip wire into the Growth Blueprint.

#### $1,500 Growth Audit: Good price, wrong framing

- **The credit mechanic is smart**: "$1,500 credited toward your project" is genuine risk reversal. The problem is the framing. It is presented as a standalone product with a nice-to-have credit rather than as an essential investment that is automatically absorbed into the larger engagement.
- **Hormozi lens**: This should be positioned as "you invest $1,500 in the Growth Blueprint. If we work together, that $1,500 disappears into your project cost -- meaning you got it for free. If we do not work together, you keep the Blueprint and use it however you want."
- **Recommendation**: Keep the price. Reframe the language. And stop calling it an "audit."

#### $5K-$15K Build & Launch: The range is a problem

- **Range signals uncertainty**: When a buyer sees "$5K-$15K," they do not think "flexible." They think "I bet it will be $15K." Or worse: "This person does not know what things cost." A $10K spread is 3x the floor. That is not a range -- it is a guess.
- **Hormozi lens**: Hormozi would say price it at the top of what the market will bear, then make the value stack so heavy that the price feels low by comparison. Do not give a range. Give a starting point.
- **Recommendation**: "Investment starts at $7,500" or "Most projects fall between $8,000-$12,000." Tighter range. Or better: eliminate the range entirely and say "Your Growth Blueprint determines the exact scope and investment -- most clients invest $8,000-$12,000." Now the Growth Blueprint is doing double duty as both a strategic deliverable and a scoping tool.

#### $800-$2,500/mo Growth Partnership: Buried and undervalued

- **Problem**: This is mentioned in a single line below the pricing cards. It reads as an afterthought. But recurring revenue is the most valuable thing a freelancer can build.
- **Hormozi lens**: This is where lifetime value lives. If a client stays 12 months at $1,500/mo, that is $18,000 in recurring revenue from one client. This deserves its own section, its own value stack, and its own bonuses.
- **Recommendation**: Either give it a dedicated fourth card or create a separate page/section for it. Include what is covered monthly (hours, deliverables, response time SLA). Name the specific outcomes: "Monthly SEO monitoring, conversion rate optimization, performance reporting, and priority support."

### The $15K ceiling question

Is $15K too high for the trust level a first-time visitor has at the point of seeing this section? Possibly. But the answer is not to lower the price. The answer is to build more trust before they reach the price.

Current page flow: Hero > Problem > About > How It Works > Services > Results > Testimonials > **Pricing**

By the time someone reaches pricing, they have seen:
- 3 case studies with real outcomes
- 4 testimonials from named professionals
- A personal bio with credibility markers
- Stats (290%, 12x, 80%)

That is actually a strong trust runway. The issue is not the price point -- it is that the pricing section itself does not reinforce any of that trust. It stands alone as a cold, transactional element after pages of warm, personal content.

## 7. Guarantee Strategy

### Current state: No guarantee

The closest thing to a guarantee is buried in the FAQ: "Then we fix it. I do not hand you a website and disappear."

This is not a guarantee. This is a reassurance. It has no structure, no conditions, no teeth. It does not reverse risk -- it just implies good intentions.

### Why this matters enormously

Hormozi: "Guarantees are the single most powerful tool for increasing conversion on high-ticket offers."

For a small business owner spending $8,000-$15,000 on a website -- possibly their largest single marketing expense of the year -- the risk is enormous. A strong guarantee does not just increase conversion. It communicates confidence.

### Hormozi's four guarantee types applied to Nice Right

#### 1. Unconditional Guarantee

"If you are not completely satisfied with the Growth Blueprint within 14 days of delivery, I will refund your $1,500 in full. No questions asked."

- **Risk to Marcin**: Low. The Growth Blueprint is a deliverable that demonstrates competence. If someone goes through the full deep-dive process and still wants a refund, they were never going to be a good client.
- **Signal to buyer**: "He is so confident in this work that he will eat the cost if it is not good."
- **Recommended for**: The Growth Blueprint (entry product)

#### 2. Conditional Guarantee

"If your new website does not generate at least [X] qualified leads in the first 90 days after launch, I will continue working on it at no additional cost until it does."

- **Conditions**: The client must (a) keep the site live, (b) not make unauthorized changes to core elements, (c) maintain the analytics tracking installed at launch.
- **Risk to Marcin**: Moderate but controllable. The Growth Blueprint phase identifies realistic targets. The conditions ensure the client does not sabotage the outcome.
- **Signal to buyer**: "He is betting on his own work."
- **Recommended for**: The Customer Growth Engine (core project)

#### 3. Anti-Guarantee

"I do not offer refunds on project work because I only take on projects I am confident will succeed. The Growth Blueprint phase exists specifically to ensure we both believe in the plan before a single line of code is written. If the Blueprint reveals that a full build is not the right move for your business, I will tell you -- and you keep the Blueprint to use however you want."

- **Risk to Marcin**: Zero. This is honest and builds trust through directness.
- **Signal to buyer**: "He is selective and honest. He would rather lose a sale than do work that will not succeed."
- **Recommended for**: Fallback position if conditional guarantee feels too risky at first

#### 4. Implied / Performance-Based Guarantee

"Your Growth Accelerator retainer has no lock-in. Month to month. If this month's work does not justify next month's investment, you walk away. I keep your business because the results keep you here."

- **Risk to Marcin**: The "no lock-in" language is already on the site. This just reframes it as a guarantee.
- **Signal to buyer**: "He earns my business every month."
- **Recommended for**: The Growth Accelerator (monthly retainer)

### Recommended guarantee stack

Layer these together:

1. **Growth Blueprint**: Unconditional 14-day satisfaction guarantee
2. **Customer Growth Engine**: Conditional 90-day performance commitment ("I keep working until it performs")
3. **Growth Accelerator**: Implied guarantee via month-to-month terms with explicit "results earn my business" language

### How to present the guarantee

Do not bury it. Hormozi treats the guarantee as a headline-level element. It should appear:

- In the pricing section, directly below the price
- As its own styled element (a badge, a boxed callout, or bold text)
- With a name: "The Results-First Guarantee" or "The 90-Day Performance Promise"

## 8. Urgency and Scarcity

### Current state

One line: "Currently taking on 2 new projects per quarter. Spots for Q2 2026 are open."

### Assessment

This is real scarcity and that is good -- Hormozi insists on honest scarcity only. But it is underdeveloped. A single line in small monospace text at the bottom of the pricing section does almost no psychological work.

### Why "2 projects per quarter" is powerful but wasted

The math: 2 projects per quarter = 8 projects per year. For a solo operator charging $8K-$15K, that is $64K-$120K in project revenue. This is a real constraint. It means Marcin literally cannot take on more. That is not fake scarcity. That is operational reality.

But the current presentation treats it as a footnote. It should be a headline.

### Hormozi's scarcity framework applied

#### Scarcity of quantity (strongest)

"I take on exactly 2 new projects per quarter. One spot for Q2 2026 is already filled."

- Be specific. "2 spots" is good. "1 spot remaining" is better. Update this in real time.
- Show the constraint is real by explaining why: "Because I work directly with every client and refuse to hand projects off to subcontractors, I cap at 2 new builds per quarter."

#### Scarcity of access

"The Growth Blueprint is the only way to start a project. I do not take on builds without completing this first."

- This creates a gate. Even if someone has money, they cannot skip the process. That signals quality and selectivity.

#### Urgency of cohort

"Q2 2026 projects kick off in April. To make the Q2 window, your Growth Blueprint needs to start by March 15."

- Give a specific date. "Spots are open" has no urgency. "Blueprint must start by March 15 to make Q2" creates a real decision deadline.

#### Urgency via pricing

"The Growth Blueprint is currently $1,500. This introductory rate is locked for projects that begin in Q2 2026."

- If a price increase is planned, signal it. Even if it is not planned yet, the option to raise the Growth Blueprint to $2,000 or $2,500 later is legitimate. Saying "current rate" implies it may change.

### Recommended urgency/scarcity implementation

Replace the current single line with a structured element:

```
Q2 2026 AVAILABILITY

I take on 2 new projects per quarter.
[1 of 2 spots remaining for Q2.]

To start in April, your Growth Blueprint needs to begin by March 15.
Book your free strategy call to reserve your spot.
```

Key changes:
- Specific number of spots remaining (update manually as projects are booked)
- Specific deadline with a reason
- The deadline is for the Blueprint, not the full project -- lower commitment threshold
- "Reserve your spot" language (scarcity + action)

### What Hormozi would add

A **waitlist** for when spots are full. "Q2 is full. Join the Q3 waitlist." This accomplishes two things:
1. Social proof (other people want this enough to wait)
2. Genuine urgency for the next cycle

Even when spots are available, showing that previous quarters were full signals demand.

## 9. The Rewrite: Recommended Offer Structure

### Principles guiding this rewrite

1. **One core offer** with a clear entry point
2. **Named and priced bonuses** that expand the value gap
3. **A guarantee** that reverses risk
4. **Real scarcity** with specific numbers and dates
5. **Outcome-focused language** throughout

### The recommended page structure

#### Section header

**Your Investment**

Turn your website into a customer growth engine -- or your Growth Blueprint fee is on me.

#### The entry point: Digital Growth Blueprint ($1,500, credited toward your project)

Frame: "Every project starts here. Not because I want to charge you twice, but because building without a plan is how businesses waste money. This is the plan."

What you get:
- Deep analysis of your business, customers, and competitors
- Custom digital growth roadmap with specific revenue projections
- Clear scope, timeline, and fixed investment for your project
- Yours to keep forever -- even if you choose a different path

Timeline: Delivered within 10 business days of our kickoff call.

Guarantee: "If the Growth Blueprint does not give you a clear, actionable path to digital growth, I will refund your $1,500 in full. No questions asked."

CTA: "Book Your Growth Blueprint"

Pricing note: "This $1,500 is credited in full toward your project. If we move forward, your Blueprint was free."

#### The core offer: Customer Growth Engine ($8,000-$12,000)

Frame: "Your website, app, or digital system -- designed and built to bring in customers, not just look good."

What is included:
- Full website/app/system designed and built on your Growth Blueprint
- Competitor Intelligence Report (value: $750) -- exactly where you can win
- 90-Day Performance Dashboard (value: $500) -- custom analytics in plain English
- 30-Day Post-Launch Tune-Up (value: $500) -- performance review and optimization
- Content Launch Kit (value: $500) -- SEO post outlines, social templates, Google Business guide

Timeline: Most projects go live within 6-8 weeks. Quick wins often show up within days.

Guarantee: "The 90-Day Performance Promise: If your new digital presence does not generate measurable results within 90 days, I keep working at no additional cost until it does."

What you contribute: One 30-minute call per week. I handle everything else.

Total value: $10,250-$14,250 (including bonuses)
Your investment: $8,000-$12,000

#### The ongoing relationship: Growth Accelerator ($1,200-$2,500/mo)

Frame: "Your digital presence is not a project. It is an asset that should keep growing."

What is included monthly:
- SEO monitoring and optimization
- Conversion rate testing
- Monthly performance report with plain-English analysis
- Priority support (24-hour response time)
- One strategic recommendation per month

Terms: Month to month. No contracts. I earn your business every 30 days.

#### The quick diagnostic: 48-Hour Revenue Roadmap ($750)

Frame: "Already have a website? Get a prioritized list of exactly what to fix and what it will do for your revenue. Delivered in 48 hours."

What you get:
- Full review of your current site with revenue impact estimates
- Prioritized list of high-impact fixes (what to do first)
- Competitor comparison snapshot
- Actionable -- implement it yourself or hire me to do it

Note: $750 is credited toward any project engagement within 60 days.

#### Scarcity element (beneath all pricing)

```
Q2 2026: 1 of 2 project spots remaining.
Growth Blueprints for Q2 must begin by March 15.
```

#### Social proof anchor (in or adjacent to pricing)

"Last quarter: 290% traffic growth for a home service company. 12x more leads from the same site."
